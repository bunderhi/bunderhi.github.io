Tuscany 2023 Trip

This trip was all about cycling the Tuscan countryside. Our homebase was Mormoraia Agriturismo in the center of Tuscany near a very cool medieval village called San Gimignano. Mormoraia is a working farm with a four star winery and olive oil production. We drank their wine and eat their olive oil every day.

Ride Pictures

Via Francigena

We accidentily found ourselves following an ancient pilgrimage path called Via Francigena on some of our rides. Via Francigena runs all the way from Canterbury England to Rome. The churchs below were refuges for pilgrims on the way to Rome. We ran into lots of pilgrims hiking the trail.

The coolest thing we found was this unmarked hidden pool just off the trail. It turned out to be "The Caldane". These are the remains of a roman bathhouse built on a warm water mineral spring. You can still still ceramic tiles on the bottom. The baths were destroyed in 1200 by invading troops. Wikipedia says locals still use the pools for their healing powers.

GIRO

Giro d'Italia

We moved our homebase to a beach town called ViaReggio for the last few days of our trip. This is so we could catch a stage of the Giro d'Italia bike race. Giro d'Italia is a 20 day stage race that is the Italian equivilent to the Tour de France. Stage 11 of the race finished and Stage 12 started in ViaReggio.

We stayed at the Hotel Plaza de Russie. Easily the most luxurious hotel we've ever visited. The breakfast experience exemplified the type of place it was. If the lady had a purse they would setup a "purse chair" for it at the table.

Here is an excerpt on the history of the Hotel -

Erected in 1871 along the route of the Grand Tour, it is the oldest hotel in ViaReggio. Situated along the seaside promenade, the hotel is in the heart of the Art Nouveau district that arose in the early 1900s. First to find shelter here were Russian expatriates, fleeing from the Tsarist Empire, followed by Russian aristocracy escaping the Bolshevik Revolution. Events such as these gave the hotel not only a certain number of accounts that were to go forever unpaid, but also the “de Russie” name, which it maintains to this day.
